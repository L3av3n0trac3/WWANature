Icaricia shasta

Common name
Shasta Blue
Family
Lycaenidae
Family common name
Gossamer Wings
On the wing
M June to M August
Biennial: first winter spent as eggs, second as nearly grown larvae.
Habitat
High elevation rocky hilltops and ridges.
Larval host:
Legumes, especially rattleweeds, lupines, milkvetches, locoweeds and clovers.
Nectar plants:
Hosts, pussypaws, alpine buckwheats, and others.
Abundance
LC
Recognition
Smaller than 1 in. Ventral hind-wing has a subtle row of metallic blue-green flecked scintillae, capped in turn by pale orange aurorae, black chevrons, and white arrows pointing toward the wing attachment site. In the middle of the ventral hind-wing are dark brown spots.
